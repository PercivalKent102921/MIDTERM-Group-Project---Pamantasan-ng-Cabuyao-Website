History

A Brief History

"The Pamantasan ng Cabuyao (PnC; also known as the University of Cabuyao) is a university in the city of Cabuyao, province of Laguna, Philippines. It was founded by then Mayor Proceso “Etok” D. Aguillo, through the enactment of Municipal Ordinance 2003-059 approved on April 16, 2003."

"Before the establishment of the Pamantasan ng Cabuyao, there was a satellite campus of the Laguna State Polytechnic University (LSPU) at the Cabuyao National High School. The LSPU was operating through a memorandum of agreement between the LSPU president and Cabuyao municipal mayor, which commenced in 1993 and ended on March 31, 2003."

History of Pamantasan ng Cabuyao

In late 2002, there was a strong protest against the leadership of the LSPC president from faculty members and students of LSPC. The protest was headed by Charlemagne G. Laviña and supported by Rosalia B. Pre, George Barundia, Christian Hernandez, Melissa Dimaculangan, and one non-teaching staff, along with some students from LSPC - Cabuyao Campus.

The protest was found by the Sangguniang Bayan of Cabuyao to be true and based on facts, with support from almost all students, most of whom were Cabuyeños. It resulted in the issuance of the Notice of Termination to the memorandum of agreement. This left the administration of the mayor with no option but to establish Cabuyao Community College.

Anticipating the closure of LSPC-Cabuyao, faculty members headed by Charlemagne Lavina and Frank Parao assisted the office of the mayor. They suggested that the Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila (PLM) would be an ideal model for the local college. Dr. Benjamin Tayabas, then PLM president, assisted the Cabuyao municipality and personally helped in the effort to set up a university.

Dr. Tayabas suggested that the name "Pamantasan ng Cabuyao" be adopted. On April 15, 2003, the Sangguniang Bayan enacted Municipal Ordinance No. 2003-059, and the ordinance was approved the following day by Mayor Proceso “Etok” D. Aguillo.

Pamantasan ng Cabuyao was inaugurated on July 31, 2003, the birthday of its founding mayor, and was personally inaugurated by President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo on June 19, 2003, during the 107th birthday of Dr. Jose Rizal.

During the term of Mayor Isidro L. Hemedes, Jr., and with the able assistance of Atty. Rommel Gecolea, who succeeded him after winning the 2016 elections, significant educational reforms were introduced. New and improved systems were implemented, and old practices were turned into sound and solid written policies and procedures.
